The Maxim Microcontrollers SDK (MSDK), now part of [Analog Devices](https://www.analog.com), gives you everything needed to develop firmware for MAX32xxx and MAX78xxx microcontrollers. It includes a GCC-based toolchain, a peripheral driver API written in C, Board Support Packages for official evaluation kits, and over 50 example projects per supported part.

## Supported microcontrollers

The MSDK supports the full range of Analog Devices MAX32xxx and MAX78xxx parts, including:

- **MAX32520** — ChipDNA secure microcontroller for IoT
- **MAX32650 / MAX32655 / MAX32660 / MAX32662** — Ultra-low-power Arm Cortex-M4 MCUs
- **MAX32665–MAX32666** — Cortex-M4 with Bluetooth 5 for wearables
- **MAX32670 / MAX32672 / MAX32675** — High-reliability industrial MCUs
- **MAX32680 / MAX32690** — Cortex-M4 with BLE 5.2 and precision analog
- **MAX78000 / MAX78002** — AI microcontrollers with CNN accelerators

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the MSDK">
    Download the automatic installer for your OS from the [Installation](/installation) page and run it. The installer bundles the GCC toolchain, OpenOCD, and all SDK resources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your development environment">
    Choose your IDE — [Visual Studio Code](/quickstart-vscode), [Eclipse](/quickstart-eclipse), or the [command line](/quickstart-cli) — and follow the quick-start guide for your platform.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open an example project">
    Navigate to the `Examples/<TARGET>/` folder in your MSDK installation, open an example for your target microcontroller, and build it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flash and debug">
    Connect your evaluation board, run the `flash` build task (or `make flash` on the command line), and launch the debugger to verify the program starts in `main`.
  </Step>
</Steps>
